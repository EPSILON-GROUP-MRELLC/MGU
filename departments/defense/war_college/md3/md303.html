Course Overview

MD303 teaches how to dominate the global chessboard through sovereign strength, strategic presence, and principled diplomacy. You'll learn how to maneuver across world systems with discipline, outlasting alliances, and commanding fear-respect without sacrificing your code. This is foreign policy for the autocrat who bends to no bloc, yet shapes them all.

Learning Objectives

  • Develop an independent geopolitical doctrine based on your state's ethos, goals, and threat matrix.
  • Negotiate from strength—whether militarily weak or economically small—using leverage, asymmetry, and clarity of purpose.
  • Control perception across borders through visible actions, narrative, and dignified defiance.
  • Construct a statecraft model that earns reluctant respect—even from enemies.

Curriculum Breakdown

  1. Geostrategy 101
    Understanding territory, trade lanes, ideological zones, and how geography governs fate.
  2. Hardline Sovereignty
    Why concessions breed contempt—and how to defend red lines credibly without escalation.
  3. Diplomacy for Autocrats
    Silent alliances, posture over pandering, and the art of earned recognition.
  4. Proxy, Pressure, Presence
    Using economic tools, information campaigns, and foreign infrastructure to shape external behavior.
  5. The Respect Doctrine
    Building a reputation that says: “We want peace, but we do not fear war.”
  6. Extraterritorial Leverage
    Operating beyond borders—legally, covertly, or commercially—while avoiding overreach.

Assignments & Exercises

  • Strategic Map: Create a full-spectrum geopolitical doctrine for your sovereign model, including allies, enemies, and leverage paths.
  • Diplomatic Letter: Draft a realpolitik communiqué that asserts red lines and sovereign posture with discipline and dignity.
  • Historical Breakdown: Analyze a non-aligned state that thrived without blocs. How did it command respect?
